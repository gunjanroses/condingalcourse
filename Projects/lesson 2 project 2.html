life and career:

Lisa was born Pranpriya Manobal on March 27, 1997,[3] in Buriram province, Thailand

Later she legally changed her name to Lalisa at the advice of a fortune teller for prosperity

An only child, she was raised by her Thai buddhist mother, Chitthip Brüschweiler, and her Swiss stepfather, chef Marco Brüschweiler

After enrolling in dance classes at the age of four, she began competing regularly in contests

It included To Be Number One and joined the eleven-member dance crew We Zaa Cool alongside BamBam of Got7.

Pre-debut activities:

In 2010 at age 13, Lisa auditioned to join the South Korean record label YG Entertainment in Thailand.

She grew interested in K-pop as a child thanks to artists such as BigBang and 2NE1 and aspired to follow a similar path.

Among 4,000 applicants, she was the only one to qualify, which prompted then CEO Yang Hyun-suk into offering Lisa a chance to become a YG Entertainment trainee.She impressed one of the judges there with her stage confidence and off stage attitude

In 2011, Lisa moved to South Korea to begin her formal training. She officially joined the label as its first non-ethnic Korean trainee on April 11. She appeared as back up dancer in one music video and she took up a modelling offer too.

2016-2023: Debut with Blackpink and Lalisa

In August 2016, Lisa debuted as one of four members of South Korean girl group Blackpink, the first non-ethnically Korean artist under the label.

As the years went on the group blackpink rose to fame and becaame no.1 girl group in K-pop

Her many solo songs reached 1b views especially Money. And she and other members started endorsing other brands collaborated with many artists.

She and her group earned world records for their music and global recognition
